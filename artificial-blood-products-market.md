<p><ul><li>Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carrier (PFBOC)</li><li>Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carrier (HBOC)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The artificial blood products market is primarily categorized into two types: Perfluorocarbon-based Oxygen Carriers (PFBOCs) and Hemoglobin-based Oxygen Carriers (HBOCs). PFBOCs utilize synthetic chemicals to transport oxygen, offering advantages like long shelf life and reduced risk of disease transmission. In contrast, HBOCs are derived from human or animal hemoglobin, designed to mimic the oxygen-carrying function of red blood cells. Both types aim to address blood shortages and enhance patient care in medical emergencies and surgical procedures.</p></p>
