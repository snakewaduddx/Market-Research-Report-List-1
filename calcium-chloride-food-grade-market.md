<p><p>The Calcium Chloride food grade market is divided into two primary types: dihydrate and anhydrous. Dihydrate Calcium Chloride contains two water molecules and is commonly used for food preservation and as a firming agent due to its ability to retain moisture. Anhydrous Calcium Chloride, devoid of water, is often utilized for its de-icing properties and in applications requiring high solubility. Both forms are essential in food processing, enhancing shelf life and improving texture and safety in various products.</p></p>
