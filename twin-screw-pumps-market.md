<p><p>Twin-screw pumps are positive displacement pumps that use two intermeshing screws to move fluids efficiently and with minimal pulsation. They are renowned for their ability to handle both low and high viscosity fluids, making them ideal for various applications in industries such as oil and gas, chemical processing, food and beverage, and pharmaceuticals. The design of twin-screw pumps enables them to provide a steady flow, control shear rates, and reduce the risk of cavitation.</p><p>The Twin-Screw Pumps Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 10.9% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for efficient fluid handling solutions and technological advancements in pump design. <p><ul><li>Sealed Twin-Screw Pump</li><li>Non-sealed Twin-Screw Pump</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Twin-Screw Pumps market is categorized into sealed and non-sealed types. Sealed twin-screw pumps feature a hermetic design that prevents leakage, making them suitable for handling hazardous or volatile fluids. They provide enhanced safety and efficiency, particularly in chemical and petrochemical applications. Non-sealed twin-screw pumps, on the other hand, rely on mechanical seals that allow for easier maintenance and cost-effectiveness. These pumps are typically used for less critical applications where fluid leakage is not a significant concern.</p></p>
