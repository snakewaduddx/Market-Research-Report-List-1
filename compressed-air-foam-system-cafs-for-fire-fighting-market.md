<p><p>Compressed Air Foam Systems (CAFS) enhance firefighting efficiency by using compressed air to mix water and foam concentrate, creating a stable foam. The market includes three main types: </p><p>1. Low-expansion foam systems produce a minimal foam volume for surface fires. </p><p>2. Medium-expansion foam systems generate moderate foam volumes, suitable for flammable liquid fires. </p><p>3. High-expansion foam systems create large volumes of foam, effective for deep-seated fires in enclosed spaces, offering extensive coverage and insulation. Each type serves specific firefighting needs.</p></p>
